What is Emotion Analysis and what does it do?

Emotion Analysis is a real-time AI-powered tool that detects and tracks facial emotions during live sessions. It identifies 7 emotions — Happy, Sad, Angry, Surprised, Fearful, Disgusted, and Neutral — through your webcam and provides an engagement score, session timeline, emotion breakdown, and AI-generated insights to help educators and learners understand engagement patterns.

Do I need an account to use Emotion Analysis?

Yes, an account is required to use the live session features. Sign up with your email and set up two security questions for account recovery. Once logged in, you get access to live detection, session history, analysis reports, and personalized insights. Admin accounts have an additional dashboard for managing all user sessions.

Which browsers are supported?

Emotion Analysis works on Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari — any modern browser with WebRTC webcam support. No plugins or downloads needed. For the best performance and most accurate detection, Chrome or Edge on a desktop or laptop is recommended.

How accurate is the emotion detection?

The model is trained on the RAF-DB dataset and detects 7 emotion classes. Accuracy is highest when your face is well-lit, centered, and at a natural angle. The confidence score shown on screen reflects how certain the model is for each frame.

Why is the live detection not working or showing errors?

First, ensure the backend server is running. Then check that camera permission is granted. Make sure your face is well-lit and fully visible in the frame. A "Weak Signal" warning means the server isn't responding; check your connection.

What is the difference between Live Cam and Detect mode?

Live Cam is a continuous session mode that tracks emotion trends over time and produces a full engagement report. Detect mode is a single-snapshot mode — you upload or capture one image and instantly get that frame's emotion probabilities.

How does the engagement score work?

The engagement score (0–100) is a weighted average calculated across all detected emotions during the session. Positive emotions contribute most. A score above 75 indicates strong engagement, 50–74 is moderate, and below 50 suggests the learner may be disengaged.

What does "Generate Analysis" do and when should I use it?

After stopping a session, click "Generate Analysis" to get a complete breakdown including Emotion Breakdown panel, Engagement Score, Key Insights with timestamps, and personalised Tips with actionable advice.

Can I scroll back through the live emotion trend graph?

Yes. The Live Emotion Trend graph retains all data points from the entire session. You can scroll left at any time to review older emotion patterns. Hovering over any point shows a tooltip with the dominant emotion and confidence percentage.

Is my camera feed stored or shared?

No. Your camera feed is never stored or shared. Frames are captured locally, sent to the backend only for analysis, and immediately discarded once the prediction is returned. No images, video, or biometric data are saved to any server or database.

Was this helpful?
What data does Emotion Analysis collect about me?

Only your account information (email, username, hashed password, and security question answers) is stored for login and recovery. During sessions, only the emotion prediction results and engagement scores are saved — not the raw video or images.

How do I reset my password if I forget it?

On the login page, click the "Reset" tab. Enter your registered email and answer the two security questions you set during sign-up. If both answers are correct, you can set a new password immediately. No email link is required.
